--- layout: archive title: "Teaching & Supervision" permalink: /teaching/ author_profile: true --- Currently, I mainly teach classes on statistical programming and statistical learning in the Econometrics Master Programme and in the Data Science and Marketing Analytics Master at Erasmus University Rotterdam. Additionally, I teach a PhD course at Erasmus Graduate School of the Scocial Sciences and Humanities. Here, I focus on the statistical analysis capabilities of the R programming environment (i.e., univariate, grouped and nested as well as multivariate models).

I have supervised numerous BSc and MSc theses on the following topics: (1) feature attribution methods (LIME, Lift, Shapley Values), (2) uncertainty quantification with probabilistic neural networks and deep ensembles, (3) Bayesian estimation of large scale/ high dimensional panel regression models. I am also the master thesis coordinator of the Business Analytics and Quantitative Marketing track of the Econometrics programme.
